VIANA DO CASTELO

Where history meets the Atlantic Ocean

ABOUT VIANA DO CASTELO

Viana do Castelo is one of the most beautiful cities in northern Portugal. Located at the mouth of the Lima River, it offers a perfect blend of seaside charm, historical richness, and cultural significance.

With its stunning architecture, from medieval to contemporary, Viana do Castelo charms visitors with its unique atmosphere. The city is known for its folk traditions, crafts, and the ornate Basilica of Santa Luzia perched on a hilltop offering breathtaking views.

GETTING THERE

By Air

The nearest international airport is Francisco Sá Carneiro Airport (OPO) in Porto, located approximately 75km south of Viana do Castelo. From there, you can:

  • Take a direct train from Porto Campanhã station (about 1h15m journey)
  • Rent a car (about 45-50 minutes via A28 highway)
  • Use a shuttle service or taxi

By Train

Viana do Castelo is well-connected by train to major Portuguese cities:

  • From Porto: Regular services throughout the day (1h15m)
  • From Lisbon: Connect via Porto (total journey ~4h)
  • From Braga: Direct services (45m)

Check the official Portuguese Railways (CP) website for schedules and tickets.

Local Transportation

The city center of Viana do Castelo is relatively small and walkable. Most attractions, the venue, and recommended hotels are within walking distance.

For longer distances, local buses operated by Auto Viação do Minho connect different parts of the city. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also readily available.

EXPLORE VIANA DO CASTELO

Top Attractions

  • Basilica of Santa Luzia A stunning church offering panoramic views of the city and ocean.
  • Old Town Explore the historic center with its narrow streets and traditional architecture.
  • Praia do Cabedelo A beautiful beach perfect for surfing and relaxation.
  • Gil Eannes Ship A museum ship that once served as a hospital vessel for the Portuguese cod fishing fleet.

Food & Drink

Viana do Castelo offers excellent cuisine focusing on fresh seafood and traditional Portuguese dishes.

  • Bacalhau à Gomes de Sá A must-try codfish dish in the region.
  • Vinho Verde The local "green wine" is light and perfect for warm days.
